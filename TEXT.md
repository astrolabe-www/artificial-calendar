This is a proposal for a series of monthly artificial astronomical charts, inspired by the astrolabe and other technologies of embodied measurement and placemaking.

While traditional sun path charts(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun_path#Angles) provide concise ways of tracking and predicting the Sun's position throughout the year, these charts map the positions of prominent artificial astronomical objects throughout each month.

Although satellite orbits are highly predictable and can be forecast well into the future, they are still predictions, much like weather predictions, because their exact paths are influenced by external factors such as atmospheric drag, gravitational forces from the Sun and Moon and solar radiation.

This superposition of natural and artificial "stars" makes explicit the different cycles and rhythms to which our bodies are subjected.

As an example, I'm attaching satellite/star charts for New York City, for March and April, 2026. It shows not only the Sun's path throughout the month, but also the Moon's path, the highest pass of the International Space Station, the highest pass of the meteorological satellite NOAA-3, the positions of the geostationary GOES satellites, and passes from the two most frequent satellites from the Starlink and Kuiper fleets.

The design is intentionally minimalist and borrows its colors from the calendars in the 2024 Almanac. Visual elements could later be adjusted to match the publication's overall design, style and aesthetics. A short descriptive text can also accompany a set of charts, or individual charts, pointing out specific peculiarities for each month.
